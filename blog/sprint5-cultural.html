Core Skills Reflection

Due the feeling directionless and disorganised for a long time, I had quiet doubts in the back of my head that I would be unsuccessful in organising my time, and getting tasks completed at all. I have been quite surprised that I can focus on a task, and stick to it without distraction. I have enjoyed having a sense of purpose again. That I can be somewhat organised and I am working towards establishing habits in meditation and journaling

I honestly thought I might be faced with a moment where I would be so overwhelmed that I just give up rather than work through and keep going. The core skills and reflecting on my values and goals really grounded me and helped me find direction. Learning mindfulness has helped me become more aware, aware of others and how I interact with them and establish consistent communication with people in my life. Previously, totally unintentionally weeks would sometimes go by without contact with friends and family

I have also learned that I haven’t lost my people skills as I had previously thought, through the listening and looping (which was a great task in itself) conversation came easily with complete strangers. This gave me confidence that I can have success with this course.

Core skills so far has been an exploration of identity that at times has been difficult, but the concepts around reflection and learning have been eye opening. As I mentioned above It is early days yet, but I hope to establish a daily habit of reflecting in a journal. With the purpose to help with my education but also as a way to establish accountability, recording what I achieved that day and tracking goals I set for myself.

Values, empathy and self awareness each play important roles in developing into a well rounded human being. Being self aware means recognizing one’s needs, values, judgments, and biases. Self awareness means being able to recognise the emotions of others, how what you are doing, how you are acting may effect those around you.

"Self-awareness allows us to see things from the perspective of others, practice self-control, work creatively and productively, and experience pride in ourselves and our work as well as general self-esteem (Silvia & O’Brien, 2004).”

Reflection is a part of being self aware and has also proven to be a valuable tool in education (and programming where you will never stop learning as code evolves over time). Reflecting on how you felt during activities, when you had success or failures, exploring why this or that happened, how it impacted you or those around you. Asking these questions of your self when reflecting helps your brain to establish strong pathways to memory as you explore a task from various angles.

What surprised me most about the core learning was how challenging reflection could be. It was difficult at first to really explore my values and motivations, do I even have a culture? Identity? This deep dive into who I am was a rewarding experience in the end.

Learning mindfulness was also a surprise. I had never done much exploration or really considered the benefits of meditation before now. I had always considered mediation a spiritual exploration or part of alternative culture previously. Now I am trying to make it part of my daily routine.

Apart from these skills being vital to being a well rounded individual, they are essential to working in a team environment. They help you to develop healthy relationships and build a strong foundation, knowing who you are and what impact you want to have on the world.

This part of the course has been invaluable to me, helping me find direction and purpose again. The topics explored each week have also been fascinating to learn about. Having a growth mindset is something I have always tried to have (whether I was always successful….?) and will try to keep in mind as I move forward with this course and in life. I definitely don’t see any of it as a waste of time
