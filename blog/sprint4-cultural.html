What is neuroplasticity?

It was believed for many years that the adult brain was essentially fixed, that 'what you had was what you got’ and there was no changing it. As well as this belief, many people are under the illusion that they are not ’smart’ or intelligent enough to pursue an area of interest. Due to this people will avoid altogether or not put their all into learning a new skill or in the pursuit of academics.

The study of the brain over the last 20 years and the discovery of Neuroplasticity has proven this belief to be a fallacy. Neuroplasticity is the the process by which your brain changes depending on what has happened to it. This relates to memory, learning, trauma and biomechanics. Our brains are essentially plastic that is constantly being reshaped through our daily experiences.

As training and exercise is essential for athletes to develop muscle memory and muscle mass. Though learning and mental exercises you are developing the nerves in your brain. It has even been proven that mental practice has the same effects on the brain, effects the same pathways in the brain, as physical practise does. This is an example of neuroplasticity at work. Imagining yourself doing a task, helps to build stronger pathways, to cement that skill or memory firmly in the brain.
