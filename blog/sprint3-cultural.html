EQ vs IQ, What is the difference?

“Emotional intelligence or EI is the ability to understand and manage your own emotions, and those of the people around you. People with a high degree of emotional intelligence know what they're feeling, what their emotions mean, and how these emotions can affect other people.”

Emotional Intelligence helps you build stronger relationships succeed in life, you career and personal goals. It enables you to be able to connect to your feelings, turn intention into action and make informed decisions about what matters most to you.

Emotional Intelligence is defined by four main attributes:

  1. Self Management - You’re able to control your impulsive feelings and behaviours, manage emotions in healthy productive ways, follow through on commitments and adapt to changing circumstances.
  2. Self Awareness - You recognise your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behaviour. You know your strengths and weaknesses, you have confidence in yourself.
  3. Social Awareness- You have empathy. You can understand the emotions, needs and concerns of other people, pick up on emotional cues, feel comfortable socially, and recognise the power dynamics in a group or organisation.
  4. Relationship management- you know how to develop and maintain good relationships, communicate clearly, inspire and influence others, work well in a team and manage conflict.

IQ is a number derived from a standard intelligence test, Scores were originally calculated by dividing the individuals mental age by their chronological age then multiplied by 100. Iq represents the a persons ability in a number of fields

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Working memory and short term memory
  • Quantitative Reasoning

EQ , is the measure of a person’s level of emotional intelligence. Referring to a person’s ability to perceive, control, regulate and express emotion.

Having a high IQ will generally mean you will be successful in academics, you will be top of your class, get the best marks in school. To be successful in life and in the world outside of school you need high EQ. Researchers have shown that our success at work or in life depends on Emotional Intelligence 80% and only 20% of intellect. While our intellect help us to resolve problems, to make the calculations or to process information Emotional intelligence (EQ) allows us to be more creative and use our emotions to resolve our problems

Our emotions(feelings) are important are they help you in a number of ways. They help with attention, memory and learning. Assist in making decisions, how we feel effects how we make decisions, they also influence our unconscious bias. (a teacher in a bad mood unknowingly marks a paper lower, than if they were in a positive frame of mind). Your emotions effect your relationships to people and products, whether you move towards a person/product is regulating by emotional signals. Your physical and mental health are impacted by your feelings, often creating a feedback loop, emotion feeding emotion causing depression in some cases.
